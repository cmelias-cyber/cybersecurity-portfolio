## Security Audit – Botium Toys

### Overview
This internal security audit was conducted for Botium Toys, a fictional retail company, as part of the Google Cybersecurity Certificate program. The audit evaluates the organization’s security controls and compliance posture using the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF), focusing on identifying risks, gaps, and areas for improvement.

### Controls & Compliance Summary
- Access controls: No  
- Encryption: No  
- Backups & disaster recovery: No  
- Firewall/IDS: No  
- Authentication controls: No  
- Compliance (PCI DSS / GDPR): No  

### Observations
The organization currently lacks several foundational security controls, which increases exposure to operational, financial, and compliance risks. Without proper implementation of access restrictions, encryption, and monitoring systems, sensitive data and business operations may be vulnerable to threats.

### Recommendation
Botium Toys should prioritize implementing core security controls, including least privilege access, multi-factor authentication, encryption for sensitive data, and formal backup and disaster recovery processes. Additionally, the organization should ensure compliance with PCI DSS and GDPR requirements to reduce regulatory and financial risk as the business expands.
