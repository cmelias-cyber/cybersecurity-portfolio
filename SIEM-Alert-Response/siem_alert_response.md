## SIEM Alert Response – Suspicious File Download

### Objective
This activity demonstrates the process of responding to a security alert generated by a Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system. The alert indicated a suspicious file download associated with a potential indicator of compromise (IOC) domain match. The response followed standard incident response procedures aligned with organizational playbooks and industry best practices.

### Alert Details
- **Alert Type:** Suspicious File Download  
- **Source:** SIEM Dashboard  
- **Indicator:** Domain match to known IOC  
- **Category:** Potential malicious activity  
- **Priority:** Investigate and validate  

### Step 1: Assess the Alert
The first step was to assess the alert by gathering additional information to determine whether the alert was valid. This included reviewing log data, examining the associated domain activity, and verifying whether the alert represented a legitimate security threat.

**Action Taken:**  
Collected relevant log details and analyzed alert context to confirm validity.

### Step 2: Contain and Eliminate the Threat
After confirming the alert was valid, containment and eradication procedures were initiated according to the organization’s incident response playbook. These steps focused on preventing further impact and removing any traces of malicious activity from affected systems.

**Action Taken:**  
Implemented containment measures and eliminated identified threats.

### Step 3: Restore Affected Systems
Once the threat was contained and removed, affected systems were restored to normal operation. Restoration ensured that services resumed securely and that system integrity was maintained.

**Action Taken:**  
Restored system functionality and verified operational stability.

### Step 4: Communicate and Document the Incident
After resolving the incident, communication with relevant stakeholders was completed to ensure awareness and coordination. The incident was documented to support post-incident analysis, continuous improvement, and future incident response readiness.

**Action Taken:**  
Reported incident resolution and documented response activities.

### Outcome
The incident was successfully identified, contained, and resolved using structured incident response procedures. The process reinforced the importance of timely alert validation, coordinated response actions, and clear communication throughout the incident lifecycle.

### Skills Demonstrated
- SIEM alert monitoring and analysis  
- Incident response workflow execution  
- Threat containment and system restoration  
- Security communication and documentation  
- Application of incident response playbooks
